Jordan Danberry

Founder

Jordan’s shared passion for community service and sports prompted her vision to inspire the next generation. The Arkansas native is a distinguished athlete and leader in her community. Jordan was fortunate to see success at an early age while earning honors such as Arkansas Gatorade Player of the Year for both junior and senior seasons at Conway High School.

Jordan initially began her collegiate career at The University of Arkansas. After leaving Arkansas, Jordan went on to play at Mississippi State University to receive numerous accolades and recognition for her academics and athletics including:

Master of Business Administration
Bachelor of Science in Business Economics
Elite 90 Award
All SEC Second and Defensive teams
Naismith Player of the Year Midseason team
Naismith Defensive Player of the Year Semifinalist

Leadership

“My time at Mississippi State showed me the type of leadership role I could play in my community.
I realized the impact I could have on young lives during my final year of college ball.”

While performing at a high level of competition, Jordan was also able to coordinate a program that focused on increasing self-esteem in young girls of color. Although only consisting of about 20 young ladies, Jordan aspired to reach even more.

“My first experience of the mentor/mentee relationship was through summer programs such as AAU basketball and youth camps.”

There are ample reasons as to why mentorship is so important. The impact of this exchange led Jordan to establish The Gem Sports Club.

“I desire to create an environment where the youth could not only play and develop in a sport that they love, but also understand the strength of self-empowerment.
I believe everyone is born a GEM but not everyone realizes their own power.”
